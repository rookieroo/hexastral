## 1. On "a logo + the name will avoid rejection" — partly true, here's the nuance

The founder's instinct (file the brand as logo + name; non-overlapping business →
no refusal) is **half-right, and the right half is what matters here**:

- **A logo does NOT immunize the word.** A §2(d) likelihood-of-confusion refusal
  compares the marks' *sound, appearance, and meaning* plus the *relatedness of
  the goods*. A design element gives a composite mark a different overall
  impression, but an examiner still reads the dominant **word** portion — so a
  logo lockup is **not** a reliable way to register a word that's confusingly
  similar to a senior mark. Worse, a composite (word+logo) mark protects the
  *word itself* **weakly** — a competitor could take the bare word in a different
  style.
- **What actually clears the YUEL cl-9 cluster is the *goods* prong, not a logo.**
  YUELEE / YUELAN etc. are Shenzhen-seller **electronics/accessories** marks;
  Yuel is **metaphysics/relationship software**. The relatedness prong is weak →
  a 2(d) citation, if raised, is rebuttable. The lever is a **tight, narrow
  goods/services identification** that pins the software field and distances it
  from the cited goods — see the drafts in §4.

**Therefore the filing strategy is: file the STANDARD-CHARACTER WORD MARK first**
(broadest protection — covers the name in any font, including the logo lockup),
with carefully-scoped IDs. A **design/logo mark is an optional *second* filing**
once the visual identity is final; it is not a substitute and not urgent.

## 5. Filing sequence (decided in ADR-0024; restated for the attorney)

1. **US first — §1(b) ITU**, both marks (YUEL cl 9/42/45; YUUN cl 9/42). This is
   the **basic application** and locks the priority date.
2. **Other four via the Madrid Protocol**, within the **Paris Convention 6-month
   priority window** from the US filing date — one WIPO application designating
   **JP / SG / MY / TH** (all five launch countries are Madrid members), single
   renewal, far cheaper than four national filings.
3. **Japan — file tight to launch, not at the window's end** (strict first-to-file
   + active squatting; the JP store listing publishes the name). **Consider a
   DIRECT JP national filing** covering both the Latin mark AND the katakana
   (ユエル / ユウン·ユーン), which also **decouples JP from "central attack"** — the
   Madrid registration depends on the US basic application for 5 years, so if the
   US cl-9 YUEL cluster forces a refusal/narrowing, the Madrid designations fall
   with it; a direct JP filing survives that.
4. **Budget option**: if counsel cost is tight, designate SG/MY/TH for **YUEL only**
   first (it has the viral surface + earliest US push) and add YUUN a round later.
   Rough order-of-magnitude: two marks × 3 classes + ASEAN ≈ the $2–6K band in
   ADR-0024; the floor is self-serve knockout + single-class US ITU filings — never
   skip clearance.
